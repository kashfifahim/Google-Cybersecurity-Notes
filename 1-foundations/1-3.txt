a security lifecycle is a constantly eveolving set of policies and standards 

CIA triad: confidentiality, integrity, and availability triad 
a model that helps inform how organizations consider risk when setting up systems and security policies 

CIA are the three foundatioanl principles used by cybersecurity professionals to establish appropriate controls that mitigate threats, risks, and vulnerabilities 

security controls are safeguards designed to reduce specific security risks 

security frameworks are guidelines used for building plans to help mitigate risks and threats to data and privacy 

security frameworks have 4 core components:
- identifying and documenting security goals 
- setting guidelines to achieve security goals 
- implementing strong security processes 
- monitoring and communicating results 

compliance is the process of adhering to internal standards and external regulations 

National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) is a U.S.-based agency that develops multiple voluntary compliance framworks that organizations worldwide can use to help manage risk 

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) and the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

FERC-NERC
The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission-North American Electric Reliability Corporation 
a regulation that applies to organizations that work with electricity or involved with the power grid 

FedRAMP, a U.S. federal government program that standardizes security assessment, authorization, monitoring, and handling of cloud services and product offerings 
provide consistency across the government sector and third-party cloud providers 

CIS is a nonprofit with multiple areas of emphasis 

GDPR is a EU general data regulation that protects the processing of E.U. resident's data and their right to privacy in and out of E.U. territory
